Growth of Mold on Sugarless vs Regular Coke

The question in short:

Does mold grow better on regular coke than on sugarless coke?

The setup:

two open glass containers
two open plastic containers
normal, sugared coke
sugarless coke
One plastic container and one glass container have been filled with regular coke and labeled with "sugar". The coke is the product "MIO MIO COLA Original" and lists the following:
  • Fat: 0 grams
  • Sugar: 11 grams
  • Salt: less than 0.01 grams
Similarly, one plastic container and one glass container have been filled with regular coke. They are not labeled. The coke is the product "MIO MIO COLA Zero" and lists the following:
  • Fat: 0 grams
  • Sugar: 0 grams
  • Salt: 0.02 grams
The plastic containers each hold 205 grams of coke. The glass containers each hold 75 grams of coke.

Conduction of the experiment:

The containers will remain stationary until mold develops. Once a container shows clear signs of mold, notes will be taken and the container will be disposed of. The experiment continues until either every container became moldy or all liquid has evaporated.

Potential problems:

  • All containers will be contaminated with dust.
  • There are differences between the two drinks other than simply the amount of sugar.
  • The amount of liquid, specifically inside the smaller glass containers might evaporate relatively quickly.
This page will eventually be updated with the findings of the experiment.
